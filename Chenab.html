Chenab Bridge Construction Details

Overview

The Chenab Bridge stands as an engineering masterpiece, a testament to human ingenuity in the face of nature's formidable challenges. Situated in the rugged terrain of the Himalayas, it's a vital link in the ambitious USBRL project. Its record-breaking height and complex construction have drawn global attention.

Detailed Construction Aspects

  • Foundation Engineering: The foundations were designed to withstand extreme loads and seismic forces. Deep bored pile foundations were used, reaching depths of over 100 meters in some areas.
  • Steel Arch Design: The arch is a two-ribbed steel structure, designed to distribute loads evenly. The steel was specially alloyed to resist corrosion and extreme temperatures (-10°C to 40°C).
  • Concrete Piers: The concrete piers supporting the arch are designed to withstand significant vertical and lateral loads. High-performance concrete was used to ensure durability and strength.
  • Fabrication and Transportation: The steel segments were fabricated in workshops and transported to the site using specialized heavy-duty vehicles and river barges.
  • Erection Methodology: The arch was erected using a combination of self-propelled gantries, cable cranes, and temporary support structures. Precision was paramount, with tolerances measured in millimeters.
  • Welding Technology: Advanced welding techniques, including submerged arc welding and flux-cored arc welding, were used to ensure the integrity of the steel structure. Non-destructive testing was conducted to verify weld quality.
  • Corrosion Protection System: A multi-layered corrosion protection system was applied to the steel, including thermal spray aluminum, epoxy coatings, and weather-resistant paint.
  • Instrumentation: Over 1,200 sensors were installed to monitor the bridge's structural behavior, including strain, displacement, temperature, and wind speed.
  • Construction Safety: Stringent safety protocols were implemented to protect workers, given the challenging terrain and extreme heights.
  • Logistics: The remote location posed significant logistical challenges, requiring careful planning and coordination of material and equipment delivery.

Construction Timeline Highlights

  • 2002: Project sanctioned.
  • 2004: Initial construction work started.
  • 2008: Construction halted due to safety concerns.
  • 2010: Revised design and safety measures implemented.
  • 2017: Arch erection commenced.
  • 2022: Golden joint installation completed, arch closure.
  • 2023: Track laying and testing.
